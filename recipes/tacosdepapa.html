Tacos de Papa

Tacos de Papa.

Tacos never dissapoint. This recepie was a favorite on weekend dinners at home. We would prep the potatoe dough days before. It was easy to cook midweek, delicious and always tastes good.

You can embelish at the end with sour cream, lettuce and tomatoe. Just don't forget the salsa. It can make all the difference.

Ingredients

  • 6 Potatoes
  • 1 cup of Shredded Cheese
  • About 20 Tortillas
  • Oil (Based on the size of skillet. Use best judgement.)
  • 1 tsp of Salt
  • 1 tsp of Pepper
  • Shredded Lettuce
  • Sliced Tomatoe
  • Sour Cream
  • Salsa

Steps

  1. Heat a pot of water and add the potatoes.
  2. Once the potatoes are soft enough to easily be punctured by a knife, drain the pot.
  3. Let the potatoes cool down till they are warm. Do not let them get cold.
  4. While the potatoes are still warm. Add the shredded cheese to the bowl along with a pinch of salt and pepper.
  5. Smash all the ingredients till they are of the consistency similar to dough.
  6. Heat a comal.
  7. Once the comal is hot, warm up tortillas. The tortillas should not be fully cooked. They should be cooked enough that they can be easily folded, but are not hard.
  8. Add a ball of potatoe dough to the tortilla and fold. Do not over stuff since this will cause the dough to melt and spill into the skillet when its cooking.
  9. Heat a skillet and oil. Once the oil is hot begin to fry the tacos in batches of 3 or 4. They are finished when they look golden all over.
  10. Garnish the tacos by opening up the taco and adding sliced tomatoes, lettuce and salsa.
